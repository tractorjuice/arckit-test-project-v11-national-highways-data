## Document Purpose

This document identifies and evaluates UK funding opportunities relevant to the National Highways Data Architecture Modernization programme. Each opportunity is scored for eligibility against the project funding profile, with particular attention to the organisation's status as a government-owned company (GovCo). Research was conducted on 7 April 2026 using live web sources.

**Important**: Grant deadlines, funding amounts, and eligibility criteria change frequently. All information should be verified directly with the funding body before committing to an application. Dates stated were accurate at the time of research.

### Eligibility Considerations for Government-Owned Companies

National Highways is classified as a government-owned company under the Companies Act 2006, operating as an arm's-length body of the Department for Transport. This status creates specific eligibility constraints:

- **SME-only programmes**: National Highways is ineligible as lead applicant (e.g., Innovate UK Smart Grants, many Catapult competitions)
- **Collaborative programmes**: National Highways can often participate as a project partner alongside SME or academic leads
- **Public sector programmes**: Eligible for programmes explicitly targeting government bodies (e.g., DSIT digital programmes, Government Cyber Action Plan)
- **Research partnerships**: Can participate via Knowledge Transfer Partnerships (Management KTPs only) and EPSRC Prosperity Partnerships
- **Internal funding**: National Highways has its own Designated Funds (£89M in 2025-26) including Innovation and Research streams

## Recommended Funding Strategy

### Top Recommendations

1. **RIS3 Designated Funds -- Innovation and Research** -- National Highways' own innovation fund provides the most direct, barrier-free funding route. The £27B+ RIS3 settlement for 2026-2031 with explicit technology renewal expectations creates a strong internal business case. Recommend positioning the data architecture programme as a flagship Digital Roads deliverable.

2. **National Data Library Programme** -- The £100M+ NDL programme is specifically designed for public sector data infrastructure. National Highways' road network data (traffic, incidents, sensor data) is a strong candidate for a "high-impact public sector dataset." Proactive engagement with DSIT in spring 2026 is critical to influence the NDL's scope and secure funding for road data integration.

3. **Government Cyber Action Plan** -- The £210M programme directly supports OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE data handling requirements for ANPR and CCTV data. As a CNI operator, National Highways is a priority beneficiary. This funding can support the security architecture, NCSC compliance, and data classification components of the modernisation programme.

4. **EPSRC Digital Roads Prosperity Partnership** -- National Highways is already a partner. The programme runs to March 2027 and can provide research outputs for Digital Twin, sensor data, and road monitoring capabilities that feed directly into the data mesh architecture.

5. **TransiT Hub** -- The £46M digital twin for transport programme offers partnership opportunities for decarbonisation monitoring data, environmental digital twins, and transport analytics research.
