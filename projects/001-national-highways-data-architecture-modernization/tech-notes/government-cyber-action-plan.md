## Summary

The Government Cyber Action Plan, launched in January 2026, is backed by over £210 million of central investment and establishes a new Government Cyber Unit led by the UK's CISO within DSIT. The plan targets improved risk identification, incident response, and recovery capabilities across government departments and public sector organisations. The accompanying Cyber Security and Resilience Bill covers transport as an essential service. For National Highways as a Critical National Infrastructure operator, this programme represents a significant funding and support opportunity for OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE data handling.

## Key Findings

- Over £210 million central investment to drive the Government Cyber Action Plan forward through phased implementation to 2029 and beyond
- New Government Cyber Unit established, led by the UK's CISO and overseen by DSIT
- Cyber Security and Resilience Bill sets clear expectations for organisations providing services to government, explicitly covering energy, water, healthcare, and transport
- Focus on reducing legacy IT as a source of cyber risk; the number of high-risk legacy systems rose 26% from 2023 to 2024
- Over 25% of the UK government's digital budget is spent on reducing legacy IT and cybersecurity
- The plan provides expert support to departments and demands "measurable progress"
- NCSC supports critical national infrastructure organisations with technical advice, guidance, assurance, and incident response
- The plan is specifically for government departments and public sector organisations (not a general business programme)
