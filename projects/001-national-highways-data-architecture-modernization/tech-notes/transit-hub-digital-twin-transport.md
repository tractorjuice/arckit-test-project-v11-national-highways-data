## Summary

The TransiT Hub is a £46 million research hub for digital twinning of the UK transport system, representing EPSRC's largest-ever investment (£20 million) in transport digital twinning. Led by Heriot-Watt University and the University of Glasgow with 67 partners, the hub focuses on using digital twins to decarbonise transport. The programme addresses key research challenges including data access, standardisation, governance, interoperability, dynamic modelling, federation, decision support, and trust/security.

## Key Findings

- Total investment: £46 million (EPSRC £20 million + contributions from 67 partners)
- EPSRC's largest-ever investment in digital twinning of UK transport
- Led by Heriot-Watt University and University of Glasgow
- 67 partners involved from academia, industry, and public sector
- Focus on transport decarbonisation through digital twin technology
- Five core digital twinning research challenges addressed: data, dynamic modelling, federation/collaboration, decision support, and trust/security/ethics
- Related UKRI programme: Core Research Challenges in Digital Twinning for Crisis Resilience (up to £4.375M FEC, co-developed by DfT and Dstl) -- though this specific call closed September 2024
- Research outputs applicable to transport infrastructure monitoring, environmental impact assessment, and predictive analytics

## Relevance to Projects

- **Project 001 (National Highways Data Architecture Modernization)**: The TransiT Hub's decarbonisation focus aligns with the environmental monitoring requirements (carbon emissions, air quality, biodiversity, noise) and the net zero corporate emissions target. The digital twin research challenges (data federation, interoperability, governance) directly map to the data mesh architecture's federated data product approach. National Highways can engage as an industry partner providing road network data and sensor infrastructure for digital twin development.
