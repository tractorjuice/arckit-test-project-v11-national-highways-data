## Summary

The CAM Pathfinder programme is a £150 million DfT/CCAV/Innovate UK initiative running until 2030 to accelerate the commercialisation of connected and automated mobility technologies in the UK. The programme is delivered through the Centre for Connected and Autonomous Vehicles (CCAV), a joint DfT/DBT policy unit, supported by Zenzic and Innovate UK. As of 2025, £18 million had been committed to 37 projects, with multiple future funding rounds expected from the remaining allocation. The UK government confirmed self-driving commercial pilots will be fast-tracked to spring 2026 on England's roads.

## Key Findings

- Total programme funding: £150 million, running until 2030
- Programme delivered by CCAV (joint DfT/DBT unit), supported by Zenzic and Innovate UK
- £18 million already committed to 37 projects in 2025
- Demonstrate strand: £20 million allocated, £500K-£2M per project, TRL 5-6 target
- Lead applicant must be a UK registered business; public sector organisations eligible as partners only
- Self-driving commercial pilots fast-tracked to spring 2026 (taxi/bus-like services without safety driver)
- DfT's Connected Vehicles Framework is approaching the "funding and pilots" stage
- Data for Road Safety (DFRS) protocol launched January 2026 for Euro NCAP integration
- Projects must improve safety, sustainability, and inclusivity of connected and automated mobility
- No single partner may exceed 70% of total eligible costs in consortium bids

## Relevance to Projects

- **Project 001 (National Highways Data Architecture Modernization)**: Directly relevant to the CAV data readiness and V2X communication requirements. National Highways can participate as a partner providing road infrastructure data, sensor networks, and real-world testing environments. The data mesh architecture's expansion to support CAV data domains aligns with CAM Pathfinder objectives. Future funding rounds from the remaining £132M+ allocation represent ongoing opportunities.
