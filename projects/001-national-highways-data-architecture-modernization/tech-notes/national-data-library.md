## Summary

The National Data Library (NDL) is a DSIT-led programme backed by over £100 million at Spending Review 2025, forming part of a £1.9 billion investment in cross-cutting digital priorities. The NDL aims to provide easy, ethical and secure access to public data assets, treating government data as a strategic national asset. A discovery phase was completed by January 2026, with further programme details expected in spring 2026. The programme supports the AI Opportunities Action Plan and targets the creation of five high-impact public sector datasets. National Highways' road network data is a strong candidate for inclusion.

## Key Findings

- Over £100 million allocated specifically for NDL creation; part of £1.9 billion DSIT digital investment over the spending review period
- Discovery phase completed as of January 2026; government will set out more details in spring 2026
- Five kickstarter projects already underway: energy bill targeting, health conditions employment support, adult social care, AI-powered legal guidance, and weather/climate data
- Chief Data Officers across government departments have been convened, with an Expert Advisory Group of independent experts from industry, academia, and civil society
- The National Underground Asset Register (NUAR) model demonstrates how infrastructure data can be shared securely across 600+ public and private sector asset owners, generating over £400 million economic growth per year
- Health Data Research Service (HDRS) receiving up to £600 million (Government + Wellcome Trust), operational by December 2030, demonstrating the scale of NDL ambition
- Strong alignment with DSIT's plan to reshape data.gov.uk as part of wider public sector data modernisation
- Government aims to create guidelines for making government datasets AI-ready
