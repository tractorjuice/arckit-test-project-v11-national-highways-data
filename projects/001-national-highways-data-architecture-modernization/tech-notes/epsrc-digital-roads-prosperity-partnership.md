## Summary

The EPSRC Digital Roads Prosperity Partnership is an £8.6 million business-led research programme (April 2022 -- March 2027) investigating how Digital Twins, smart materials, data science, and robotic monitoring can create a connected physical and digital road infrastructure system. The partnership brings together Costain Group, National Highways, and the University of Cambridge, with the Department for Transport and Digital Roads spinout Didimi joining in January 2024. The project is complemented by the £5.9 million FUTUREROADS programme (EU MSCA COFUND) providing 26 experienced researcher fellowships.

## Key Findings

- £8.6 million total programme funded by EPSRC (part of UKRI)
- Partnership: Costain Group, National Highways, University of Cambridge
- DfT and Didimi (Digital Roads spinout) joined as partners in January 2024
- Research period: April 2022 to March 2027
- Focus: Digital Twins, smart materials, data science, and robotic monitoring for roads
- Aims to replace drawings and static models with digital versions that identify when maintenance is needed
- Complemented by the £5.9M FUTUREROADS programme (26 researcher fellowships over 60 months)
- By end of programme in 2026/2027, will deliver research publications, conference papers, and strategic documents to the road industry
- National Highways is an existing partner, providing direct access to research outputs

## Relevance to Projects

- **Project 001 (National Highways Data Architecture Modernization)**: National Highways is already a named partner. Research outputs on Digital Twins, data science, and sensor monitoring directly feed into the data mesh architecture design, particularly the Assets data domain. The programme's predictive maintenance research supports the asset lifecycle data management requirements for the £157.4 billion infrastructure asset base.
